About me

I'm a Ph.D student at The University of Tokyo. My adviser is Hiroshi Imai.

My research topic is to develop efficient algorithms for Artificial Intelligence by exploiting non-extact search. In particular, my Ph.D. Thesis is about a new methods for opening books in Computer Go.

I love to get involved in ideas development contests such as Imagine Cup and Start-Up Weekend. And I am addicted to MOOCs in Coursera, Udacity, EdX and Code School.
